Sukhna Lake

Sukhna Lake in Chandigarh, India, is a tranquil reservoir nestled at the base of the Shivalik Hills. Created in 1958 by damming the Sukhna Choe, a seasonal stream, the lake faced heavy siltation from the stream's direct inflow. To solve this, a 25.42 square kilometer area in the catchment was reforested, and by 1974, the Choe was diverted to bypass the lake, reducing siltation. This thoughtful re-engineering turned Sukhna Lake into a picturesque oasis, perfect for birdwatching and leisurely strolls along its scenic paths.

Activities at Sukhna Lake

  • Sunset Watching: Experience a mesmerizing sunset over Sukhna Lake.

  • Photography: Capture stunning views of the lake, vibrant reflections, and diverse birdlife.

  • Morning Walks and Jogging: Enjoy peaceful walks or jogs around the lake.

  • Birdwatching: During winter, Sukhna Lake becomes a haven for migratory birds.
