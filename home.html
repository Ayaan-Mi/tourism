Chandigarh - The Beautiful City

Chandigarh, the capital city of both Punjab and Haryana, is often referred to as "The Beautiful City." Known for its modern architecture and abundant greenery, Chandigarh is India's first planned city post-independence, designed by the Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier. The city's layout features wide boulevards, neatly organized sectors, and a harmonious balance between urban and natural elements. The Capitol Complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a testament to Le Corbusier's modernist vision, with its impressive buildings like the High Court, Secretariat, and Legislative Assembly, all designed with striking concrete forms and spacious plazas.


Chandigarh offers a range of attractions that appeal to various interests. Sukhna Lake, a man-made reservoir, is a popular spot for boating, jogging, and enjoying scenic views. The Bird Park nearby is home to a diverse array of bird species, providing a tranquil escape for nature lovers. The Rock Garden, created by Nek Chand, is a unique artistic marvel, featuring sculptures made from recycled materials. Rose Garden, Asia's largest, showcases thousands of rose varieties and hosts the annual Rose Festival.


For shopping and entertainment, Sector-17 is the city's bustling commercial hub, while Elante Mall is among the largest shopping malls in Northern India. Art enthusiasts can explore the Government Museum and Art Gallery, which houses a vast collection of Gandharan sculptures, Pahari miniatures, and contemporary Indian art. Together, these attractions highlight the diverse experiences that make Chandigarh a captivating destination for tourists.
