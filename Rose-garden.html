Rose Garden

Zakir Hussain Rose Garden in Chandigarh, India, is a lush 30-acre botanical garden home to 50,000 rose bushes of 1,600 species. Established in 1967 and named after India's former president, Zakir Husain, it's Asia's largest rose garden. Beyond roses, the garden also features trees with medicinal properties, including bel, bahera, harar, camphor, and yellow gulmohar. With its beautiful lawns and flower beds, it's a delightful spot for nature lovers and rose enthusiasts alike.

"Things to known about the Rose Garden"

  • Among the famous tourist places to visit in Chandigarh, Zakir Hussain Rose Garden is one such attraction that draws people's attention from all walks of life including flower enthusiasts, nature-buffs, and photographers.

  • Spanning an impressive 30 acres (12 hectares), the garden is a horticultural wonderland, boasting over 50,000 rose bushes from more than 1,500 unique varieties. Here, you'll find everything from delicate miniature roses to grand hybrid teas, not to mention floribundas and climbing roses that cascade with vibrant blooms

  • As you stroll through the garden, you'll be greeted by a symphony of colors—from striking reds and radiant yellows to soft pinks and ethereal whites. Each step offers a new burst of hues and fragrances, turning the garden into a living kaleidoscope that's a feast for the senses.

  • Beyond its stunning visuals, the garden is a peaceful oasis where visitors can relax and unwind. It's a favorite spot for families, couples, and solo wanderers, who come to enjoy leisurely walks, peaceful picnics, or simply bask in the tranquil beauty of the surroundings. The Zakir Hussain Rose Garden isn't just a place to see—it's a place to experience, a vibrant celebration of nature's artistry.
