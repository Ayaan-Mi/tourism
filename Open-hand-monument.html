Open Hand Monument

  • The Open Hand Monument is a symbolic structure designed by the architect Le Corbusier and located in the Capitol Complex.

  • It is the emblem and symbol of the Government of Chandigarh and symbolizes "the hand to give and the hand to take; peace and prosperity, and the unity of mankind".

  • It serves as a venue for hosting an annual festival called festival of gardens, a major cultural event during Feb or March.

  • it stands 26 metres (85 ft) high. The metal structure with vanes is 14 metres (46 ft) high, weighs 50 short tons (100,000 lb), and was designed to rotate in the wind.

The Open Hand Monument: Symbol of Unity and Peace in Chandigarh's Modernist Landscape

  • Chandigarh's Open Hand Monument is more than just a landmark—it's a powerful symbol that encapsulates the city's spirit and modernist legacy. Designed by the iconic architect Le Corbusier, this striking monument embodies ideals of peace, unity, and openness, representing a city that is as much about forward-thinking concepts as it is about aesthetic beauty.

  • The monument features a massive hand with an open palm, mounted on a rotating metal base that sways gently with the breeze. This fluidity in design is more than just visually captivating; it signifies the dynamic nature of cultural exchange and the continuous flow of ideas, reinforcing the ethos of giving and receiving that underpins Chandigarh's identity.

  • Visitors to the Open Hand Monument find themselves immersed in a setting that juxtaposes the grand, modernist architecture of the Capitol Complex with the natural beauty of the Shivalik Hills. It's a place where artistic innovation meets philosophical depth, inviting reflection on the principles of openness and harmony.

  • Standing in the shadow of this iconic structure, you can almost feel the philosophy of Le Corbusier come alive. It's a reminder that architecture can transcend brick and mortar, becoming a medium for conveying profound messages. The Open Hand Monument speaks to all who visit, its message of unity and peace resonating with both locals and tourists.

  • For those exploring Chandigarh's rich tapestry of culture and architecture, the Open Hand Monument is a must-see. It encapsulates the city's forward-looking ethos, serving as a reminder that openness and unity are timeless values. As you stand beneath the open palm, you become part of a larger narrative—one that celebrates exchange, collaboration, and the enduring spirit of humanity.
